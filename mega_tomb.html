IBBANKATUWA MEGALITHIC TOMBS

The ibbankatuwa megalithic tombs is an ancient burial site located near ibbankatuwa wewa in galewela DS, sri lanka.The site is thought to belong to the megalithic prehistoric and protohistoric periods of sri lanka and is considered one of the several ancient burial sites that have been found in the country.Ibbankatuwa is an early iron age burial and habitation site located a village called ibbankatuwa in thalakiriyagama near the 90th mile post on the kurunegala road. Geographically it is situated on the upper reaches of the kala oya basin. Ibbankatuwa prehistoric burial site which covers an extent of approximately 1SQ km further. Ibbankatuwa megalithic burial is one of the largest and best preserved ancient burial grounds in sri lanka. Ibbankatuwa ancient cemetary dated back to the first millennium BC, they iron age agrarian village settlement associated with this ibbankatuwa burial ground belongs to the settlers and farmers of the proto and early historic period. The early iron age communities established their settlements in the ibbankatuwa area due to several positive factors which are directly affect to their day to day life especially geographical facts.


ARCHEOLOGICAL FINDINGS OF IBBANKATUWA MEGALITHIC CEMETERY
There are 42 clusters of cist tombs. each tomb made out of a floor slab, four upright stone units and a capstone forming a rectangular chamber, these stone slabs were used for the cist tombs are broadly regular in shape with a rough finish. two cist tombs, of which the capstones were intact, have been archaeologically investigated. It has yielded terracotta urns containing cremated human remains and rich finds of grave articles in the form of pottery and beads of high craftsmanship. symbols have also been found inscribed on some of the capstones.

IBBANKATUWA NECKLACE & POTTERY WORKS
The pottery and terracotta beads which were found inside the cist tombs depicts the pottery usage and terracotta technology while the beads reflect rich artistic sensibilities, social differentiations, symbols of wealth, status and power, and the imported items such as carnelian beads which are indicators of foreign trade associated with these settlement associated with this setlement are a number of micro tanks and riverside marshes, which are the potential sites to investigate early irrigated rice cultivation in the island. Archaeplogists believe that the settlement of ibbankatuwa associated with this cemetery formed the social and economic infrastructure which sustained the early buddhist monastery at dambulla and that the titled. donors of its monastic rock-shelter residences were from amongs the elite of these hinterland farming communities. Therefore, this site is historically linked closely to the dambulla monastic complex. one of the clusters of cist tombs of this cemetery have been conserved and laid out, while the artifacts recovered during excavations are displayed at the sigiriya museum.

RELICS AND PRESENT-DAY EXCAVATION
Artefacts that have been found in the premises range from clay pots, iron, copper, gold objects, beads and necklaces. intriguingly, some of the gemstones that have been found on the beads of the neckless are not found naturally in the island. making it probable to speculate that the ancient people of sri lanka engagedin international trade with neighbouring countries.
