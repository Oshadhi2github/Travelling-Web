NATURE ATTRACTION//PIDURANGALA

Pidurangala rock is a towering rock popular as a hiking spot which is situated a few kilometers north of sigiriya. It is a suitable destination for moderate hikes and climbs to the top of the rock and popular panoramic views. especially of sigiriya along with the sigiriya rock fortrees. There is againt reclining buddha statue about halfway up the rock, which is partially reconstructed out of brick. At the base of the rock is the pidurangala sigiri rajamaha viharaya. which is a white temple by the road. Whilst king kashyapa was building the sigiriya rock fortrees in the 5th centuray. he moved with monks living around sigiriya. to a new monastery on the pidurangala rock. the hike to pidurangala is more hallenging than the sigiriya rock and should not be attempted by those with poor physical fitness.


The royal cave tempe is survived only by a few remains. A rock wall still stands to show the foundation of what was once a rock temple. we missed it on the way up in the dark but stopped to check it out on the way down. A huge reclining buddha, lays inside the temple and we also didn't stop to take it in on the hike up but it was unmissable on the way down. It has been reconstructed with bricks but much of the original structure remains intact.


After you reach the temple there are only 5-10 minutes of trekking remaining. However, this part of the trail involves some bouldering. while it may seem easy to many it might be a bit challenging for some. There are no dangerous sections just a few boulders, which require a big step up. Pidurangala rock is adjacent to sigiriya rock and the best viewing spot of sigiriya rock as it is only slightly lower. pidurangala is a popular spot for sunrise as you can enjoy 360 degree views of not only the valleys but also sigiriya rock. while sigiriya rock is rich in history and has some pretty dramatic features such as the lion's paw and the mirror wall, pidurangala rock has a much more natural feel,despite also husing a temple.
