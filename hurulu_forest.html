NATURE ATTRACTION//HURULU FOREST RESERVE

The hurulu forest reserve in sri lanka is a thriving eco system eith a variety of creatures. It was designated as a biosphere reserve in 1997 . The forest reserve is a very important habitat of the sri lankan elephant . The animal is known for its migratory behavior and does so especially in the dry season between the forest situated around the area.The hurulu forest reserve represents the dry-zone evergreen forests sri lanka. there are many other protected area situated around hurulu fores reserve. Ritigala strict nature resrve,minneriya-girithale and Mahaweli flood plains nature reserves. There is no record of the number of people who live in this forest reserve. cultivation is their main means of living. In recent times the expansion of human, settlements and forest elearance have resulted in a human-elephant clashes, translocation is the conventional solution taken to solve the issue. The annual average temperature of the nature resreve is approximately around 27.3 celsius and the area receives 1600mm of rainfall annually. A distinct dry season of three to six months persists during the months april/may to september. The elevation of the area ranges from 90m to 150m above sea level.
